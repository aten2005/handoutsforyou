Some Basic Definitions

  • Microeconomics - Examines the functioning of individual industries and the behavior of individual decision-making units—firms and households.
  • Macroeconomics focuses on the determinants of total national output. Macroeconomics studies not household income but national income, not individual prices but the overall price level.
  • It does not analyze the demand for labor in the automobile industry but instead total employment in the economy
  • Aggregate is used in macroeconomics to refer to sums. When we speak of aggregate behavior, we mean the behavior of all households as well as the behavior of all firms
  • Sticky prices are prices that do not always adjust rapidly to maintain equality between quantity supplied and quantity demanded.
  • In macro economics, prices are not so flexible

Macroeconomic Concerns

Output Growth

  • Economies tend to experience ups and downs which are called the business cycle
  • The main measure of output growth is the aggregate output
  • Recessions are periods during which aggregate output decreases
  • A prolonged and long recession is called depression
  • The period of growing from a low point to a high point is called an expansion or boom
  • The period from a peak to a trough is called contraction, recession or slump
  • Sometimes the expansion phase can be longer than the contraction phase

Unemployment

  • Unemployment rate is the percentage of labor force that is unemployed and is a key indicator of the economy’s health
  • Because this is closely related to the aggregate output
  • How economy adjusts when the unemployment rises beyond a base level
  • The existence of unemployment seems to imply that the aggregate labor market is not in equilibrium that something prevents quantity supplied and quantity demanded from equating

Inflation and Deflation

  • Inflation is an increase in the overall price level
  • Keeping inflation low has long been a goal of government policy
  • Hyperinflations are periods of very rapid increases in the overall price level
  • A decrease in overall price level is called deflation

Components of Macroeconomy

  • It is helpful to divide macroeconomy into 4 groups
    • Households - Private
    • Firms - Private
    • Government - Public
    • Rest of the world - Foreign

Circular Flow Diagram

  • Cast payments made by government to people who do not supply goods, services or labor in exchange for these payments are called transfer payments
  • Examples include social security benefits welfare payments etc.
  • Household earns money and can decide to save or dissave money
  • Firms sell goods and services and earn revenue which flows into the firm sector
  • Firms pay wages, dividends etc which flow out of the firms
  • Government collects taxes but buys goods and services from firms and households

Three Market Arenas

Goods and Services Market

  • Households and government purchase goods and services from firms in this market
  • Firms also purchase goods and services from each other
  • Supply by firms
  • Demand by households, government and firms

Labor Market

  • Interaction here takes place when firms and government purchase labor from households
  • Supply by households
  • Demand by Firms and Government
  • Also supplied and demanded to from the rest of the world

Money Market

  • Aka financial market
  • Supply of funds by households
  • Demand of funds by households
  • Firms also borrow to earn more and return the money
  • When a firm, a household, or the government borrows to finance a purchase, it has an obligation to pay that loan back, usually at some specified time in the future.
  • Most loans also involve payment of interest as a fee for the use of the borrowed funds.
  • When a loan is made, the borrower usually signs a “promise to repay,” or promissory note, and gives it to the lender.
  • When the federal government borrows, it issues “promises” called Treasury bonds, notes, or bills in exchange for money.
  • Firms can borrow from banks, or in the case of corporations, they can issue corporate bonds
  • Instead of issuing bonds, corporations can also issue shares
  • A share of stock is a financial instrument that gives the holder a share of the firm’s ownership and therefore right to share the firm’s profits
  • Dividents are portions of the firm’s profits that the firm pays out each period to it’s shareholders

Role of government in Macroeconomy

Fiscal Policy

  • Government’s decisions about how much to tax and spend
  • Government collects taxes and spends these funds on goods and services
  • An expansionary fiscal policy is a policy where taxes are cut or government spending increases
  • Contractionary is the opposite

Monetary Policy

  • Controls short term interest rates in the economy

Stagflation

  • Situation of high inflation and high unemployment

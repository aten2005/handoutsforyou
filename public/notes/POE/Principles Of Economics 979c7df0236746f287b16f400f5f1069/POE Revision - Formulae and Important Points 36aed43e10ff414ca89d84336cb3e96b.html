1. Scope and Method of Economics

  • Opportunity Cost is the full cost involved in choosing a specific choice that includes what you give up when you leave the best available alternative
  • Sunk Costs, Efficient Markets
  • Positive economics does not present any opinion but Normative economices presents someone’s opinion on something and might include words like “should”
  • Efficent when we produce what the society wants
  • Equity when everything is fair
  • Economic growth is measure of increase in total output of a country
  • Post Hoc Fallacy - B happened after A doesn’t B happened due to A
  • Fallacy of Composition - If it happened to A, doesn’t mean it’ll happen to a bunch of A

5. Elasticity

  • When they say ARC elasticity use mid point formula even if direction is given.
  • If direction is given and it’s not given to use ARC elasticity then use the initial condition formula
  • Price Elasticity of demand = Percentage change in demand/Percentage change in Price
  • Cross Price Elasticity of Demand = Percentage change in demand of X/Percentage change in price of Y
  • Income Elasticity = Percentage change in demand/Percentage change in income
  • Dead weight loss smaller in inelastic goods

7. Profit Maximizing Firms

  • Profit = Total Revenue - Total Cost
  • Production function - relation between inputs and price
  • MP_k/P_k = MP_l/P_l
  • MRTS = dK/dL = -MP_L/MP_K = -P_L/P_K
  • d/dL f(L, K) = MPl and d/dK f(L, K) = MPk
  • Pl*L+Pk*K = Total Cost

8. Short Run Costs

  • Marginal Cost MC = d/dq TVC = d(TVC)
  • Average Cost = TVC/qI
  • Total Revenue = P * Q
  • To maximize profit MC = MR and for Perfectly competitive MR = P, because in PC, each firm has an elastic price
  • They can ask TVC/TFC questions and we compare using MC and MR
  • If MC < MR profit
  • If MR < MC loss

9. Long Run Costs

  • Concept is mostly same but they might ask profit as well which can be calculated from graph
  • Loss and Profit concept changes
  • Here the it depends on Total Revenue - Total Variable Costs
  • This is called operating profit
  • This is because is this term is +ve, then it need not exit from the market
  • But if it’s -ve then it’ll exit from the market
  • Minimum ATC should be lower than Price else it has to shutdown
  • Long Range Curve is drawn between scales and costs per unit
  • Long Run Equilibrium P = SRMC = SRAC = LRAC

Profit Maximization

  • MR = MC to solve for q
  • and P = MC to give p
  • MR = d(rev)/dq
  • MC = d(cost)/dq

Shutdown

  • Profit is zero
  • P = AVC
  • but P = MC
  • MC = AVC for shutdown

Long Run Equilibrium and Breakeven

  • Profit = TR - TC

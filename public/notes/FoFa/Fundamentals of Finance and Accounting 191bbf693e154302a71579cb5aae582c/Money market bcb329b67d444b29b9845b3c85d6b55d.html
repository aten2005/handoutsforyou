Introduction

  • Short term debt instruments are issued by economic agents that require short term funds
  • Money markets trade in secondary trade markets
  • Capital markets serve similar function for market participants with excess funds to invest for periods of time longer than one year
  • Reallocate the fixed amounts of liquid funds available in the market at any particular time
  • Need arises because of the immediate cash needs of individuals, corporations and governments do not coincide with their receipts of cash
  • Excessive holdings of cash balances involve a cost in the form of forgone interest → opportunity cost
  • We generally put only minimum needed to meet their day to day transactions
  • Holders of cash invest excess cash funds in financial securities that can be quickly and relatively costlessly converted back to cash when needed with little risk of loss of value over the short investment horizon
  • Money markets enable large amounts of money to be transferred from suppliers of funds to users of funds for short periods of time both quickly and at low cost to the transacting parties
  • Money market instrument provides an investment opportunity that generates higher rate of interest than holding cash but it is also very liquid and has relatively low default risk
  • 3 Basic charecteristics
    • Sold in large denominations - to avoid time waste and low transaction cost for the sellers
    • low default risk - the risk of late or non payment of principal and or interest is generally small
    • Original maturity of one year or less

Discount Yields

  • Some money market instruments are sold on a discount basis
  • Instead of directly received interest payments over the investment horizon, the return on these securities results from the purchase of the security at a discount from it’s face value and receipt of face value at maturity
  • These use a 360 day year
  • i_bey = i_dy * (Pf/Po) * (365/360)

Single Payment Yields

  • Pay interest only once
  • Receives a terminal payment consisting of interest + the face value

Money Market Securities

Treasury Bills

  • Main tool used by US government to implement monetary policy
  • Sold through an auction
  • Because backed by the US government, virtually default risk free
  • The risk free asset in the US
  • Little interest rate and liquidity risk
  • In competitive bids, highest bidder receives the first allocation of T bills and subsequent bids are filled in decreasing order of the bid
  • No bidder can legally receive more than 35% of the T-bills involved in any auction
  • In noncompetitive bids, the bidder indicates the quantity of T-bills he or she wants to buy and agrees to pay the lowest price of the winning competitive bidders
  • Sold on discount basis
  • Coupon rate is 0

Federal Funds

  • Short term funds transferred between financial institiutions
  • Usually for a period of one day
  • Use excess reserves for the same
  • The overnight interest rate → federal funds rate
  • Single payment loans - they pay interest only once, at maturity
  • Take the form of short term unsecured loans
  • We take 360 day year
  • Bank that finds itself with $75 million excess reserves can call it’s correspondent banks to see if they need overnight reserves

Repurchase Agreements

  • An agreement involving sale of securities by one party to another with the promise of ubuying it back at a specified price in the future
  • Essentially a collaterized fed funds loan
  • A reverse repurchase agreement is buying something with the promise of selling it back in the future
  • A given transaction has both repo and reverse repo rate
  • NOTE: In india, repo rate is very important, when RBI increases repo rate, the interest rate on money lent to banks increases and the same for consumers increases so spending decreases so liquidity decreases since loan price increases
  • We use a 360day year

Commercial Paper

  • Unsecured short term promissory note → to raise short term cash
  • Often to finance working capital requirements
  • One of the largest money market instruments
  • There is no active secondary market for commercial paper
  • It is also unsecured debt
  • A letter of credit backing commercial paper effectively substitiutes credit rating of the issuer with credit rating of the bank
  • Better the credit rating, lower the interest rate on the issue
  • Also uses 360 day year

Negotiable Certificates of Deposit

  • A bank issued time deposit that specifies an interest rate and maturity date and is negotiable in the secondary market
  • Bearer instrument - whoever holds it when it matures receives principal + interest
  • Purchased using mutual funds - group of people pool fund and buy

Banker’s Acceptances

  • Used to fund expenses which haven’t been delivered it
  • Used in international trade transactions and the underlying LOCs are used for goods that have not yet been shipped
  • Risk - very low

Market Participants

US Treasury

  • Raises significant amounts of funds in the money market when it issues T-bills
  • Most actively traded money market securities
  • Raise money to meet unavoidable Short term expenditure needs prior to the receipt of tax revenues

Federal Reserves

  • Hold T-bills when it wants to increase the money supply and selling T-bills when it wants to decrease money supply
  • Uses repurchase agreements and reverse repos to temporarily smooth interest rates and the money supply
  • Also uses a discount window

Commercial Banks

  • Participate as issuers and/or investors of almost all money market instruments discussed above
  • Banks are the major issuers of NCDs, banker’s acceptances, fed funds and repurchase agreements

Money Market Mutual Funds

  • Money market mutual funds purchase large amounts of money market securities and sell shares in these pools based on the value of their underlying (money market) securities (see Chapter 17 ).
  • In doing so, money market mutual funds allow small investors to invest in money market instruments

Brokers and Dealers

  • First group → 18 Primary Government Security Dealers
    • Marketing new issues of T-bills
    • Buying securities from the Federal Reserve
    • Assist when it uses repo market to increase/decrease supply of bank reserves
  • Second group → Money and Security Brokers
    • Use this group as intermediaries
    • Also play a major role in linking buyers and sellers in the fed funds market and assist secondary trading in other money

Euro Money Markets

  • Dollar denominated deposits held offshore in US bank branches overseas and in other banks are called eurodollar deposits
  • Market is named Eurodollar market
  • Not subject to US bank regulations
  • As an alternative to the Eurodollar market, companies can also obtain short-term funding by issuing Eurocommercial paper.
  • Eurocommercial paper is issued in Europe but can be held by investors inside or outside of Europe.

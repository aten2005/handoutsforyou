Presentations to Persuade

  • Persuasion is an act or process of presenting arguments to move, motivate and to change

Functions

  • Stimulate
  • Convince
  • Call to action
  • Increase consideration
    • We want to increase the chances for the person to look into what you are saying
    • They should change the position
    • They are open and willing to listen to what I am saying
    • I am more concerned about making them more open and considerate to what they are saying
  • Develop tolerance of alternate perspectives
    • They should be willing to agree to multiple perspectives
    • Different tools and variables
  • Need to get reinforcement
  • You need to find common ground
  • I want other person to do something new
  • I want other person to do something in my favor

Principles of Persuasion

  • Reciprocity
    • Give and take policy
    • It always starts with giving
    • Is there something I can learn and understand
  • Scarcity
    • Something rare, unusual and exclusive
    • Will help you persuade the person
  • Authority
    • All about expertise and skill
    • Always useful to persuade the person
  • Commitment and Consistency
    • We need to show our efforts and reputation, character
    • Once you commit something, you should be able to it
  • Consensus
    • Accepted by large group of people
    • If we use that kind of approach, it’s easy to convince the other person
  • Liking
    • If you as a speaker, have the ability to make the listener
    • You should make the listener feel good about themselves

Organizing Principles for Presentation

  • Time
  • Biographical → only applicable when we are talking about people
  • Comparison - emphasis on similarity → larger set than contrast
  • Contrast → talking about differences
  • Space → how parts contribute to the whole
  • Ascending and Descending
  • Cause and Effect
  • Psychological → understanding the needs of the audience
  • Problem and Solution → focus on the need and how we can solve it
  • Elimination → remove unnecessary things
  • Classification → Categorical
  • Oral Interpretation

Persuasion

  • Process of creating, reinforcing or changing people’s beliefs or actions
  • When using persuasion, you can change, instill or intensify your listener’s beliefs, attitudes and/or behaviors
  • It is also something you can learn → art as well as skill
  • Involves elements like trust and understanding human nature

Methods of Persuasion

Credibility

  • Called as ethos
  • It is the degree to which listeners believes a speaker
  • Factors of credibility involves competence and character
  • Competence refers to knowledge, expertise
  • Character refers to sincerity, trustworthy ness → how genuine he is
  • Types of credibility
    • Initial Credibility → before the process
    • Derived Credibility → during the process
    • Terminal Credibility → post the process
  • Tips for enhancing credibility
    • Explain your competence
      • you should have the ability to display your capabilities
    • Deliver your speeches fluently, expressively and with conviction

Evidence

  • Logos refers to any attempt to appeal to the intellect, the general meaning of logical argument
  • The major elements of logos are evidence and reasoning
  • Includes → case studies, examples, expert opinions, facts, rebuttal and counterargument
  • Testimonials → statements by the user
  • Case studies → example for the product
  • how they infer is reasoning
  • Use specific evidence, novel evidence
  • Use evidence from credible sources
  • Make clear the point of your evidence
  • You can go without evidence also but it’s better to go with them also

Reasoning - Mainly for Negotiation

  • It is the process of drawing a conclusion on the basis of evidence
  • Reasoning from specific instances
  • Reasoning from principle
  • Casual reasoning
  • Analogical reasoning

Appealing to Emotions

  • It is called pathos
  • Is related to the words, pathetic, sympathy and empathy.
  • It is the argument by emotion, generating sympathy or feelings for your cause or position
  • Medical insurance not emotions
  • Life insurance emotions
  • Counselling is emotional

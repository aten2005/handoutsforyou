Training Process

Identifying training needs

  • Who are to be trained?
  • How?
  • Look at performance appraisal for every person
  • For every employee, annual appraisal takes place and based on this we can check the training requirement of an individual employee
  • We can have written tests, records, attitude surveys

Methods

  • Organizational Analyses
  • Task Analyses
  • Individual Employee

Plan how to meet this need for training - Design

  • Planning, Designing, Deciding Training Modes, Deciding Training Methods, Implementing, Evaluating
  • Planning → any training program should clearly have objectives and expected outcomes
  • Set overall training objectives, decide training modes(on the job, off the job), assess training times, construct the timetable, arrange trainers and decide teaching methods, Determine control systems required
  • There are some training programs you cannot do while on the job
  • Deciding the teaching methods could include - lecture, talk, job instruction (talk, demonstrate and practice), discussion, project, case study, role play etc
  • Discussion has free flow of information
  • Lecture is more formal and talk’s interaction level will be higher
  • In tray is a reflection of an average day of an employee

Implement the training program

  • We decided whom to train, who will train and what the training will be on
  • You need to ensure some flexibility in the training program

Evaluating the Training Program

  • Evaluation is the assessment of total value of a training program in performance, learning and financial terms
  • Levels of evaluation
    • Reaction → rating and how they would recomment
    • Learning → have they learnt the topics maybe use quizzes and small questions
    • Behavior → application of knowledge
    • Results → what has it resulted in, for the organization, for the individual

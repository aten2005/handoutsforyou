Introduction

  • The major part of HR cost goes in training and recruitment and they intend that the employee should stay back in the organization
  • Starting a new job → most stressful experience for any employee
  • As manager you should ensure you reduce this joining stress
  • We inform the new employee his duties and responsibilities, salary, perks, do(s) and don’t(s) of the organization
  • Induction + Orientation + Socialization
  • Tech companies also use the term onboarding
  • Induction is the process for introducing a new employee to his work environment
  • Formal introduction for the employee

Induction vs Training

  • Induction deals with familiarizing the employee to the environment he is going to work in
  • Training has a wider scope and includes imparting skills and technical knowledge
  • If too much information is shared, there’s no point of the whole process since the audience gets overwhelmed
  • Training is about a skill
  • Campus: Formal system for map

Importance of Induction

  • Improves motivation
  • Assimilates the workplace culture
  • Provides knowledge of the products/services
  • Boosts confidence
  • Improves work quality and productivity
  • Reduces incidences of early leaving
  • Disseminates health and safety rules

Goals of Induction

  • Helps new employees settle in
  • Helps new employees understand their responsibilities and what is expected of them
  • Ensures that the new employee starts contributing as early as possible
  • If he doesn’t know what he has to do, it’ll be hard for him to deal with work

Who should receive Induction?

  • New employees
  • Existing staff who have been promoted, transferred or returned after long absence
  • Long term temporary staff

Stages of Induction

Pre Induction

  • Job advertisement
    • Don’t overpromise
    • Be realistic - design and copy that reflect the culture of the organization
    • Emphasize on aspects of the work that current employees find satisfying
    • Accurate about pay, terms and any special conditions that may apply
  • Application pack
    • Provide literature about the organization
    • Suitable job description
    • Brief outline of terms and conditions
    • Norms that are not part of the contract
  • Pre employment handbook
    • Friendly and jargon free
    • Arrangements for the first day
    • The time and place the employee should arrive
    • Parking or transport arrangements at site
    • The name of the person whom they should meet
    • What type of clothing should be worn
    • Specific security arrangements they should be aware of
    • The catering facilities that are on site and near by
    • List of documents they will need to bring on the first day
    • An outline of the program for the first day

Primary Induction

  • The day the employee joins you
  • Should address both the inductee’s and the employer’s immediate needs and priorities
    • Health and Safety
    • Conveniences
    • Personal details
    • Details of the next of kin → general practitioner
    • Important documents the employee needs to receive from the organization
  • By the end of day 1
    • Location of work site
    • Time recording procedure
    • Rest/meal break times
    • Health and Safety rules
    • Location of their personal work station
    • Rules about PC/phone
    • Key points of conduct
    • Their immediate supervisors and colleagues

Main Induction

  • Working norms
  • How to get along with people
  • Long process

Designing an Induction Program

Factors to be Considered

  • Target Audience
  • Essential and desirable information - you decide
  • Literacy level of employees

Strategies

  • Formal or Informal
  • Individual or Collective → mostly with group of people and not 1:1 manner
  • Technical or Non Technical

What should we tell them?

  • Organization
  • Work environment
  • Job instruction
  • History and structure
  • Benefits and holidays
  • Products and services, safety procedures

Who should tell them?

  • Immediate supervisor
  • Training or personnel department
  • Top management
  • Department heads
  • Specialists
  • Nominated peer/buddy
  • Customers/suppliers/distributors

Evaluating the Induction Program

Primary Evaluation

  • The reaction, learning and behavior of the inductee
  • We might ask quiz or something and check the answers

Secondary Evaluation

  • The effects of the induction on the organization
  • How much people are leaving, if people actually reduce leaving
  • To allow them to settle as early as possible → how much it has contributed

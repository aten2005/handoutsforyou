Introduction

  • Trust is on the decline and easy to take for granted
  • Trust is the foundation of leadership
  • Trust is a competency that can be learned, applied and understood
  • Trust is something that you can get good at, something you can measure and improve
  • People think trust is something intangible

What is Trust and Trustworthiness

  • Ways to talk about trust
    • Act of trusting
    • Characteristic or trait of the one who is trusted
    • Quality of the relationship between people
  • Trust is the degree to which people believe in other’s abilities, motives, reliability and integrity
  • Trustor and Trustee → One who is being trusted is trustee and the one who carries out the act of trusting is trustor
  • Trustworthiness is always about the other person
  • You should not complain that someone is not trusting you or something
  • Trust is based on two factors
    • Aptitude
      • Degree to which I believe you are capable and competent
    • Benevolence
      • Degree to which I believe you care about me and will back me up

Elements of Trustworthiness

  • T = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) / Self Orientation

High Credibility

  • Believability
  • Heavily Driven by words
  • What you say and how believable you are to others?
  • Words, Skills, Credentials, expertise and experience
  • Cognitive Characteristic → Critical Thinking, Logic, Reasoning Ability
  • If you give a word, it’s fine you don’t keep your word but you should handle it carefully
  • Be good at what you are and resolve problems

High Reliability

  • Dependability and consistency
  • Actions we take and how dependable we appear
  • Predictability
  • Requires time
  • Comes from multiple experiences with that person
  • Consistency → Follow up and be consistent and responsible
  • Admit when you are wrong

High Intimacy

  • All about emotions
  • Being sensitive to others
  • Ability to connect on a human level
  • Others feel understood and will share easily
  • They should feel safe and secure when you are around

Low Self Orientation

  • Are you focusing on your own needs
  • Refers to personal focus
  • Selfishness and self obsession
  • High levels of self orientation mean low levels of trust worthiness
  • Be sincere, be honest and be respectful

Behaviors of High Trust Leaders

Character Based

  • Talk Straight → Tell the truth, let people know where you stand, use simple language and don’t manipulate people or distort facts
  • Demonstrate Respect → Treat everyone with respect, especially those who can’t do anything for you and genuinely care for others
  • Create Transparency → Be open and authentic, don’t have happen agendas and don’t hide information
  • Right Wrongs → Apologize quickly. Don’t cover things up and don’t let pride get in the way of doing the right thing. Make compensation where possible
  • Show Loyalty → Give credit freely. Represent others who aren’t there to speak for themselves. Don’t bad mouth others behind their backs and don’t disclose others’ private information
  • One argument could be ki they might become overconfident but the main point here is that the employee gets confidence that you are valuing him and will volunteer for work from next time

Competence Based

  • Deliver Results → Get the right things done, make things happen but don’t over promise and under deliver. Don’t make excuses for not delivering
  • Get better → Be a constant learner. Develop feedback systems - both formal and informal. Act on the feedback you receive.
  • Confront Reality → Address the tough stuff directly, acknowledge the unsaid but don’t skirt the real issues.
  • Clarify Expectations → Disclose and reveal expectations. Don’t assume that expectations are clear or shared. Don’t violate expectations
  • Practice Accountability → Hold yourself and others accountable. Don’t avoid or shrink responsibility
  • Listen First → Listen before you speak. Listen with your ears and your eyes and heart. Don’t presume you have all the answers or all the questions
  • Keep commitments → Say what you are going to do. Then do what you say you are going to do. Don’t break confidences. Sign or symbol of honor
  • Extend Trust → Extend trust abundantly to those who have earned your trust and extend it conditionally to those who are earning your trust. Learn how to appropriately extend trust to others based on the situation, risk and credibility

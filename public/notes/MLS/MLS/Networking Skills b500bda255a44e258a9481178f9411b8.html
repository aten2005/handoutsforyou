Types of Networking

Operational Networking

  • Linked to functional tasks
  • Assists in managing day to day responsibilities
  • Building good working relationships with the people who can help them do their jobs
  • The purpose of this type of networking is to ensure coordination and cooperation
  • Links are governed by routine, short term demands

Personal Networking

  • Personal development
  • Building their knowledge in domains beyond their areas of proficiency
  • Largely external to the organization
  • Made up of discretionary links to people with whom we have something in common

Strategic Networking

  • Relationships and information sources that collectively embody the power to achieve personal and organizational goals
  • Developing new business opportunities
  • Use indirect influence
  • A good strategic network is leverage i.e. the ability to marshal information, support, and resources from one sector of a network to achieve results in another
  • Difficult for emerging leaders because it absorbs a significant amount of time and energy

Steps to build a better network

Preliminaries

  • Identify your goals and needs
  • Distinguish what you have to offer a network
  • What is that you bring to the table

Analyze

  • Start by looking at the individuals in your network
  • Classify your relationships by the benefits they provide:
    • Information
    • Potential support and influence
    • Personal Development
    • Personal support and energy
    • A sense of purpose or worth
    • Work/life balance → good to have someone who manages everything

De-Layer

  • Creating a space with people whom you don’t want for some reason
  • In that space, you can bring in new people
  • Make some hard decisions about which relationships to back away from
  • Minimizing contact with people who sap you of energy or promote unhealthful behaviors
  • Which of the six categories have too many people in them
  • Consider which individuals and types of people as determined by function, hierarchy or geography have too much of you and why?

Diversify

  • Fill with the right people
  • Focus on positive, energetic, selfless people
  • Think about how you could connect your network to your personal and professional goals
  • Draw a physical map of your potential network

Capitalize

  • Make sure you’re using your contacts as effectively as you can
  • Can people in one sphere, could be used to fill a need in another?
  • Could you get more out of some relationship you put more energy into them?

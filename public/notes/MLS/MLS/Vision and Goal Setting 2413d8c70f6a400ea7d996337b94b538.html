Introduction

  • The leader wants to make a difference
  • Chart a direction that will motivate people
  • Leader should develop a clear and compelling picture of the future and to secure commitment to that ideal
  • Three elements (vision, alignment, execution) are essential keys for leadership effectiveness - both in yourself and in others
  • Modern organization is composed of specialists, each with his or her own narrow area of expertise, its purpose must be crystal clear
  • Only focused and shared vision will hold an organization together and enable it to produce

What is Vision?

  • A vision projects a clear image of a possible future
  • Vision is an ideal image of what could and should be
  • Mental journey from the known to the unknown
  • Creating future from a montage of current facts, hopes, dreams, dangers and opportunities
  • Vision is the force which molds meaning for the people of an organization
  • Becomes guide or roadmap to a better future
  • Differentiates true leaders from mere managers
  • Should generate energy and enthusiasm

Components of Vision

  • Ideal - Highest aspiration
  • Unique - Distinctive and extraordinary and should be your identity
  • Image - Vision should be conceptualized
  • Future Oriented - Should set a direction and stretch your mind into future
  • Common good - bring people together, shared sense of destiny
  • Questions to ask to test
    • Is this the right direction? — If destination is wrong, then no point of all the components
    • Are these the right goals? — If vision is not clear in leader’s mind, it’ll be blur in the follower’s mind
    • Is this the right time?

Interacting Elements of Vision

Organizational Vision

  • Involves a comprehensive picture of the existing system within it’s env
  • How action in one area may affect other area
  • Provides a base for a future vision
  • Cognitive skills are essential for the development of organizational vision

Future Vision

  • How organization will look at some point in the future
  • Not possible to use rational planning models to envision the future
  • Requires a leap of imagination that cannot be totally supported by forecasts of figures
  • Draws on the personal values beliefs of leaders
  • Synthesizing an appropriate direction for an organization is very complex. Several dimensions of vision is required
    • Foresight, hindsight. perception, process of revision and depth perception

Personal Vision

  • Identify your own personal resources and position themselves to play to their strengths
  • Requires self-awareness and the ability to identify, mobilize and coordinate complimentary skills and resources
  • Select situations that allow leaders to do what they do well
  • Leaders recognize their limitations

Strategic Vision

  • Connecting the reality of present(org vis) to the possibilities of the future(fut vis) in a unique way(per vis) that is appropriate for the org
  • Use org resources
  • managing the change process
  • requires the patience to live in and endure over the long term
  • Strengthen supporting mechanisms

Sharing the Vision

  • Vision has to be shared with everybody in the organization
  • Communicate the vision to others in ways that will help them understand it, remember it and then go on to share it themselves

Ways to communicate (A lot)

  • Stay on the sunny side → how do we use things to our advantage

Goals

  • Derived from vision
  • A written dream
  • Long term → 3+ years
  • Generalized

Objectives

  • Derived from goals
  • Specific results that an organization seeks to achieve
  • State direction, aid in evaluation and reveal priorities
  • Objectives can be parallel or sequential

Types

Strategic Objectives

  • Entire organization
  • Duration of medium(1-3) and short term(<1)

Financial Objectives

  • Financial wellbeing of your organization
  • Primary Objective is Growth → Stability → Growth → Consolidation → Growth(3)
  • Consolidation ensures that you become stable for a while before growing again
  • Survival + Growth 🙂

Elements

  • Clear → should be same for everyone
  • Concise → should be crisp and precise
  • Specific → if it’s not specific becomes goal
  • Realistic → Challenging and achievable
  • Specific Measurable Actionable Realistic Time → SMART → try to meet all attributes

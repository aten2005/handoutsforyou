What is a Team?

Introduction

  • A team is a group
  • Has a definite membership with interdependence, group consciousness, a sense of shared purpose and an ability to act in a unitary manner

Interdependence

  • Extent to which members rely on other members of the same team
  • Task
    • They cannot complete the task without the other team member’s help
  • Goal
    • Goals of the entire team vs the goals of the individual teams
  • Outcome
    • Whether there is a work or a punishment and how it’ll be shared within the team
    • Some might have performed but some might not have performed but how do we split the outcome works
    • Interdependence comes in the fact that some might not work but still will take credit

Types of Task Interdependence

  • Pooled
    • Members make separate, independent contributions to overall team performance
    • Group performance is the sum of member contributions
    • Work doesn’t flow between members of the team
    • Members depend on each other because they belong to the same organization or department
    • There is no direct interdependency
  • Sequential
    • Members perform tasks in a sequential order (a predetermined order)
    • Interaction occurs only between members who perform tasks that are next to each other
    • Work flows from one member to another member in one direction
    • The level of each member’s performance consequently affects the performance of other members down the line.
    • Therefore it is hard to determine individual performance
  • Reciprocal
    • Work performed by a member is dependent on work by others.
    • Members share information and work closely together
    • Work flows from one member to another member in both directions → bi directional → back and forth
    • Mutual adjustment is necessary between the members of the team
  • Comprehensive
    • Fully connected communication network
    • Each member has a great deal of discretion in terms of what they do and with whom they interact in the course of the collaboration involved in accomplishing the team’s work
    • Each group member’s activities precede and are required for all other group member’s activities

Group vs Teams

  • In team, leadership roles are rotated
  • In team we work together but in a group the leader might not work and the leader might escape
  • Groups don’t like destructive while teams should see them as opportunities
  • If you are running short of time, use groups
  • When you need to make a decision for which we have SOPs and it’s a recurring decision → group is better
  • We use a team when
    • the job is complex
    • decision making needs to be collective → you are not confident of making the decision on your own
    • consensus is important
    • problems are broad
    • support is needed
    • individual strengths and weaknesses are difficult to identify
    • individuals are unwilling to take necessary risks
  • We should NOT use team in the following
    • issue is unimportant
    • individuals don’t want to participate
    • individual risk preferences are too high
    • time is of the essence
    • group norms are unacceptable
      • respecting each other → in a team → should not get roles in between in terms of superiority and inferiority

Types of Team

  • Formal
    • Horizontal Team → committees and task forces
    • Vertical Team → level by level order of people
  • Directed or Self Managed
    • Highly mature team
    • Team itself can take care of it’s operations
  • Virtual Team
    • Members are located geographically disperse
    • People coordinate and work using primarily technology
  • Global Team
    • Members have different nationalities and represent different globalities
    • You should have nationality driven teams

Building and Managing Teams

Determining team size and choosing members

  • Depends on the expected outcome of the team, volume of task
  • Two members - di-ad
  • Three members - tri-ad
  • Any team which has even numbers is not a good idea → for a decision → we will have even splits
  • Very large team is also not a good idea → there will be sub teams within the team
  • You should always look for complimentary splits → different kinds of skills to the table
  • Good idea who have initiative and capability + enthusiastic
  • Also good idea to rotate people → same names coming in the group lists

Defining the team’s assignment

  • What is the issue to be dealt with? What is its scope?
  • What is the team’s responsibility?
    • To perform a specific task
    • To make a decision
    • To exchange information
  • What are the constraints on the group?
    • What are its deadlines?
    • What is its budget? What other resources are available to it?
    • What should be the format of it’s final report or the nature of its final product?
    • Will it have to give progress reports?

Planning the team effort

  • Divide the team’s overall assignment into parts
  • Estimate the time and resources
  • Take necessary actions to reduce gaps

Managing the team

Norms - what a team member should do and not do

  • Prescriptive - what you should do
  • Proscriptive - what you should not do

Roles

  • Clearly define what is expected of an individual in a given task
  • Role ambiguity is not good
  • Role overwhelming → too much expectations from a particular role
  • Sometimes they can be role conflicting
  • We get role stress in these cases
  • Task oriented
    • Initiators
    • Information seekers
    • Opinion givers
    • Energizers
  • Relationship oriented
    • Member satisfaction
    • Harmonizers
    • Compromisers
    • Encouragers
    • Expediters
  • Self oriented
    • Personal gain
    • Blockers
    • Recognition seekers
    • Dominators
    • Avoiders

Stages

  • The underdeveloped team - Forming
    • unclear objectives
    • there is curiosity, excitement etc.
    • low involvement
    • poor listening → everyone wants to speak
    • workplace is for work
    • getting to know each other exercise
  • The experimenting team - Storming
    • wider options considered
    • personal feelings raised
    • more listening
    • conflicts due to openly sharing views and stuff
  • The consolidating team - Norming
    • methodical working
    • settling down in terms of processes to be formed
    • agreed procedures
    • constant review
    • greater amount of trust
    • problem solving skills
  • The mature team - Performing
    • high flexibility
    • appropriate leadership (also rotated)
    • development a priority (team and every individual member)
    • no hierarchy
    • communication role will be very developed
    • here team leader is facilitation
    • people can rotate the task

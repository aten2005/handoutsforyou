Introduction

  • Stress can be defined as our mental, physical, emotional and behavioral reactions to any perceived demands or threats
  • S = P > R → occurs when pressure is greater than resource
  • Stress is not what happens to someone - those outside forces are the stressors - but how a person reacts to what happens
  • Everyone, everything will cause stress, difficulty arises only if you cross the optimal stress number
  • No expectations leads to boredom
  • Too much stress → burnout → depression
  • You can’t live without stress nor with too much stress → balance your stress-performance ratios

Signs and Symptoms of Stress

  • We have to figure out the warning signs and the red flags
  • Behavioral → you become violent, angry and withdrawal
  • Emotional → about feelings - anxiety, fear, fatigue, depression
  • Cognitive → respect on your inability to comprehend or understand
  • Physiological → headaches, sweating, increased heart rate
  • Somatic Symptoms → Nervous system, you cannot attribute these things to stress that easily

Types of Stress

  • Survival Stress - when your nature of survival is threatened, maybe you would have faced during COVID
  • Internally generated stress - Most stress are something the person believes to be stress, poor relationships etc
  • Environmental and job stress - Created by the work space, bad leadership, unethical work practices
  • Fatigue and overwork - prolonged work, continue fatigue leads to stress

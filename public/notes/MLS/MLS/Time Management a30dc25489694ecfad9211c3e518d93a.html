Introduction

  • Two major objectives
    • Increase productivity
    • Increase quality of life
  • Life is not only about work
  • What do you do in your life? Where do you put your priorities are important for you?

Perkinson’s Law

  • Work expands to fill the time available
  • Managers tend to spend time in amounts inversely related to the importance of their tasks

Murphy’s Law

  • If anything can go wrong, it will
  • Nothing is as simple as it seems
  • Everything takes longer than you think
  • Always assume everything will go wrong and plan accordingly

Peter Principle

  • People tend to rise to their level of incompetence

Pareto Principle

  • 80% time expended yields 20% of the results

Principle of Calculated Neglect

  • Despite their apparent urgency, some problems if left alone, will go away

Approaches to Time Management

Increase amount of available time each day

  • Can lead to fatigue

Do more work in available time - pack more work in your day

  • Burnout
  • Stress

Do only the important work in the time you have available

  • you prioritize based on what is important and what is not important
  • Ones which are important are what you need to do

Setting the Objectives

  • Work: objectives given by the boss
  • Work: own personal agenda at work
  • Home: family and one’s self
  • Write your goals down
  • Refresh them everyday
  • Set dates for your goals
  • Have a plan of how to get there
  • Enjoy the process

Prioritizing

Urgency

  • Defined by other people’s need for the completion of that job
  • When is the task to be done

Importance

  • How much time should be allocated to the task?
  • independent of urgency
  • Often, importance is defined by our internal judgement

Real Crisis

  • Need to be done now and done thoroughly
  • These type of jobs should be rare
  • If they are more → you have problem of time management
  • If they are more it would be because of your inability to distinguish what is important

Can wait but certainly needs to be done

  • These are self development tasks
  • These are most easily neglected and procrastinated
  • Almost real crisis could have been handled as this type
  • Action: put these jobs into routine so that they get their share of time

Other People’s work

  • The tasks that land on our desk each day
  • Helpful on maintenance of our lives
  • Give the feeling of importance though they are not
  • Action: Spending minimum time or delegate them

Not important not urgent

  • Irrelevant jobs
  • Action: Should only be done when everything else is finished
  • Exception: fun jobs → because some fun is necessary if you need to remain effective the rest of the time
  • Take a nap → 15min

Ideal Composition

Procrastination

  • Understand what and why of your procrastination tendency
  • Remember last time what was the gain to do it in time → gives us the motivation
  • Understand how much time it takes to do a task
  • Do not be a last minute decision taker
  • Gather information for a task in advance
  • Majority do only closer to deadline

How to beat procrastination

  • Write a list of all major jobs and put it on the wall
  • Break large jobs into chunks
  • Write a daily jobs-to-do list on the previous evening
  • Put the unpleasant jobs at the top of jobs to do list
  • You can also combine jobs which are close to each other
  • Set a deadline and declare it or promise it
  • Consider delegating the job → if it’s hard for you to start things, delegate it and carry on their work. This does not mean you give the whole thing to someone
  • Plunge in and get started
  • Visualize taking action and completing the task
  • Reward yourself → drastic reinforcement
  • Self talk → say to yourself ‘I love doing the tough jobs first’
  • You figure out what makes you not start or delay things

Time Wasters

  • Being too much of a perfectionist
  • Chatting or drinking coffee
  • Distractions - Can happen but should not go too far
  • Having to do other people’s jobs
  • Irrelevant tasks for the boss
    • Handling boss is one of the hardest tasks
  • Long telephone calls
  • Talkative people
  • Waiting for people who are late
  • Walking from place to place → depends on size of organization
  • Checking mails, checking messages → fixed time

Resisting Interruptions

  • Make yourself harder to interrupt
  • Stop them before they start
  • Reduce their duration
  • If you know someone is trying to take your time → say ki you are busy and you are going somewhere and tell ki we can discuss later
  • Telephone ringing shouldn’t distract you completely
  • You will lose the flow and focus

Time management Systems

  • Action list
  • Reminder notes → kumar pro management
  • Reward yourself
  • Get a friend to help you
  • Use the diary as a reminder

Summary

  • Plan the day
  • Set goals and work towards achieving them
  • Do not postpone them
  • Identify the time waster and resolve to eliminate them
  • When feasible, delegate
  • Don’t let paperwork pile up
  • Add times for relaxation and recreation in your schedule

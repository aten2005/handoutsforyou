Introduction

  • Periodically evaluate the performance of employees. Organizations can have a formal system for performance appraisals. There’s a set template, timelines, etc.
  • Regardless of the formal system, you should provide the employee with a feedback on your own
  • It’s a very complex area. It is the process of evaluating how well employees perform their jobs when compared to a set of standards and then communicating that information to those employees
  • Personnel/Potential Appraisal implies a stock taking of the quality and quantity of the potential performance of an employee → this is a confidential exercise
  • Objectives of appraisal
    • Measurement of Performance → what they have done
    • Measure of Potential →
    • Basis for rewards
  • Helps the manager to make certain decisions → promotions, succession planning
  • Once I know strength and weakness → career development and training parts

Identifying and Measuring Employee Performance

Information Types

  • Job knowledge, work output, sense of responsibility
  • Ability to learn, Interpersonal relations, leadership
  • Team player, ability to take decisions, ability to develop subordinates
  • Communication skill, Ability to plan and organize
  • Initiative, Creative, Emotionally Stable, Personal get up, Attendance

Potential Criteria Problems

  • Deficient → you leave the variable which is actually important in that job role
  • Contaminated → you use irrelevant information with respect to that job
  • Objective → you are only using that information which is measurable
  • Subjective → intangible measures

Performance Standards

  • They define the expected levels of performance and are benchmarks, goals or targets
  • They should be realistic, measurable
  • Employees should know what is being used
  • Ratings
    • A → outstanding, consistently high performer, indeed rare
    • B → above average, meets job requirements much more than can be expected
    • C → adequate, meets job requirements as expected
    • D →
  • There will be some committee which calibrates all these grades for uniformity
  • Biases also can always be there, there can be unconscious and conscious → if unconscious, the boss can tell the manager and can check

Informal vs Systematic Appraisal

  • Informal → necessary to provide someone with feedback you do, else you don’t → day to day working relationship → communicating daily → very useful in order to avoid surprises in the formal appraisal
  • Most companies have annual appraisal

Who Conducts Appraisals?

  • Supervisor rating of subordinates
  • Employee rating of managers
  • Team or Peer Ratings (For development purposes)
    • In group assignments, the prof won’t know how the group is working and this group is evaluated on the basis of the output in general
  • Self Ratings → how do you rate yourself. In most of these you will find that the person is not honest. Who will know where they are weak and where they are strong
  • Outside Raters

Methods of Appraising Performance

  • Category Rating Methods
    • Performance related categories
    • Say planning, delegation, are some of these categories
    • Time management → we use graphic rating scales
    • Checklists also are part of category rating
  • Comparative Methods
    • Ranking
    • Forced Distribution → bell shaped curve → General Electric developed this
  • Behavioral or Objective methods
    • Behavioral Rating Approaches → Behaviorally Anchored Rating Scales - BARS
      • Anything should be measurable → cannot be like “hard working”
    • Management by Objectives → MBO
  • Narrative
    • Critical Incident → Incident + how the employee responded to it
    • Essay → descriptive answer with open ended questions
    • Field Review → primarily you get info from who are external to your organization

Rater Errors

  • Problems of varying standards
    • If rater varies the standard of measure for different people
  • Recency Effect
    • Rater tends to give more weightage to those things that have happened in the recent times
  • Central tendency, leniency and strictness errors
    • central tendency is when a lot of reviews and ratings are in a small range
    • leniency is everyone is good
    • strictness is opposite and everyone is bad
  • Rater bias
  • Halo effect
    • rating and reviewing just because of one characteristic
  • Contrast error
    • Comparing with others but it is not by design
    • It has to be by design

Appraisal Feedback Interview

Not only talking about the good part

  • Tell and Sell
    • The rater is competent
    • The ratee is willing to change their behavior and accept
  • Tell and Listen
    • As a rater I will share why I have given that kind of rating
    • You also provide an opportunity to share their views
    • You allow them to respond to the rating or evaluation → this will drain out negative emotions
  • Problem - Solving
    • Here the boss will be a facilitator
    • He is like a helper
    • He will simulate the employee to think on his own
  • Mixed Model

Evaluating an appraisal interview - At the beginning

  • Did the supervisor create open and acceptant climate
  • Was there agreement on purpose and process of interview
  • Were both parties equally prepared? - for evaluator: notes etc.

During the Interview

  • To what extent did supervisor try to understand employee
  • Were broad and general questions used at outset?
  • We need to understand why the employee has fallen short
  • Was supervisor’s feedback clear and specific
  • Did supervisor learn some new things-particularly about deep feelings and values of subordinate
  • Did subordinate disagree and confront supervisor
  • Did interview end with mutual agreement and understanding about problems and goals for improvement?

Appraisal Outcomes

  • Did the session motivate the subordinate
  • Did it build a better relationship
  • Did he or she come out with a clear idea of where he or she stood?
  • Did supervisor arrive at fairer assessment of subordinate?
  • Did subordinate learn something new about supervisor?
  • Did subordinate have clear idea of what actions to take to improve performance
  • IDP - individual development plan

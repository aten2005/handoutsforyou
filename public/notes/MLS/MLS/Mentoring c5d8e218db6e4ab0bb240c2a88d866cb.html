Introduction

  • A process where someone with more experience helps guide the learning and growth of someone with less experience
  • A mentor is someone who has a combination of experience, knowledge, and skills, and who answers questions, shares ideas, and helps guide someone with less experience or knowledge
  • Mentoring can be formal or informal
  • What is it → advising, teaching, showing
  • What it is not → “I will do it for you”

Types of Mentoring

One to one Mentoring

  • Develop a personal relationship between the mentor and mentee
  • You need a lot of mentors for this though

Group Mentoring

  • Learn from mentor as well as the peer group

Training based Mentoring

  • Develop the skill needed for that particular position

Executive Mentoring

  • Create a culture of Mentoring in the organization
  • Developing a set of managers to follow mentoring
  • But for this they need to be trained and this needs mentoring too

What is Coaching?

  • It is unlocking a person’s potential to maximize their own performance
  • It is helping them to learn rather than teaching them
  • A coach is not necessarily an expert in the staff’s speciality
  • What it is → facilitating, guiding, feedback, synthesis
  • What it is not → advising, therapy, fixing

Types of Coaching

  • Career Coaching
  • Performance Coaching
  • Skills coaching
  • Newly assigned leader coaching → coaching for a newly assigned leader so that he’s on par with the details generally in key positions to tell them their responsibilities → CXO level
  • High potential or developmental coaching
  • Coaching to provide feedback debriefing and development planning
  • Target behavioral coaching
  • Legacy coaching → CXO level → they will leave in some time, coach them to ensure they make a good imprint or impression
  • Succession Coaching → when people leave in key positions → looking for replacement from within the organization → they have potential to come up to the new position
  • Business Coaching → you are going to help organization to do their business better
  • Executive Coaching → middle and above
  • Team Coaching → for a group of people in order to build a team that is highly collaborative and effective
  • Group Coaching → improvement in leadership

Mentoring vs Coaching

  • Coaching is a subset of mentoring

Grow Coaching Model

  • Goal - Where do you want to go and what’s your goal
  • Reality - What’s the reality and where are you right now?
  • Options - What are your options to bridge the gap between your goal and reality
  • Will - What will you do to plan your next steps

Introduction

  • Includes major and minor (not much thought)
  • Atleast 2 parties. if there are more then multiparty negotiation
  • Mutually acceptable solution → one party should not force terms and conditions
  • Give and take bargain → exchange
  • Agreement reached should be implemented
    • Follow it in spirit

Preconditions

  • Issue, need and conflict will be discussed
  • Willing/have scope to reach agreement → be open and honest
  • Give and Take policy
  • Trust and commitment for the win

When not to negotiate

  • For sure, nothing to gain as it involves loss of time, money and resources. Rather surrender and hope for good faith later
  • Other party or counterparty is using unethical, illegal, immoral demands
  • Personal or company code of conduct → personal choice
  • Bad faith → can’t trust them
  • if you are unprepared for a major negotiation

Negotiation Styles

Cooperative/Interrogative - BLUE HAT

  • win win
  • each party negotiating gains maxed
  • it prioritizes not clashing
  • larger no of issues
  • diversity in interests and needs
  • could be exploited

Distributive - RED HAT

  • zero sum gain
  • one party gains and the other loses
  • needs not considered, only gains that are personal are considered
  • also ensures that the other party is harmed
  • competitive
  • self centric

Miscellaneous

  • Concession is something you give to the counterpart
  • If mode 2 is used by counterpart on a small concession, then go to 2, if they go with 1, use it as well
  • PURPLE HAT → do what the opponent does
  • Possible to shift modes based on issues, your priority of issues

Process

  • Goals, outcomes, counterpart (their strengths, weaknesses), needs and objectives
  • Drafting agenda, where to conduct → somewhere neutral
  • 1-1/team based negotiation

Ground Rules

  • Norms to be set → team size, principle negotiator
  • How long, how many breaks
  • Written or Verbal Agreement

Preliminaries

  • Brief, get to know, reiterate ground rules
  • Proposals → tentative, open minded and suggestive
  • Counterpart may seek for clarification
  • Justify what they are asking for

Exchange clarified

  • Proposal → offer → if then format → conditional

Closure

  • All negotiations might not result in this

Do(s)

  • Initiative (balance)
  • Not to keen but not too much disinterested also
  • Conceal your emotions
  • Show respect
  • Collaborate
  • Be reliable
  • Your homework

Don’t(s)

  • Under what conditions (preparation) you should walk away or break off
  • Triumph → counterpart might withdraw or be less open to further negotiations
  • Be afraid of risk
  • Reopen closed issues
  • Be afraid to break off negotiations
  • Express your intent before you need to
  • Rush to judgement

Introduction

  • Work at all levels has 3 components
    • Planning
    • Doing
    • Controlling
  • Amount of time to be applied for each increases/decreases with movements across levels
  • At the last levels, high levels of doing takes place → managing intervention comes down and SOPs are implemented
  • At the top first level, we have a lot more control → There’s no specific SOP

What is Delegation

  • It is entrusting authority and responsibility to someone to complete a clearly defined and agreed task under one’s supervision while retaining his overall authority and responsibility for the success of the work.
  • Assigning tasks to subordinates
  • One reason for not delegating → insecurity and fear that junior becomes senior
  • Always downwards
  • We delegate task and authority → responsibility comes automatically

Authority vs Responsibility vs Accountability

  • Authority without responsibility has no meaning → leads to arrogance
  • Parity Principle - Authority and responsibility should be equal
  • Authority never diminishes - Even after giving authority, you won’t lose your current authority
  • Flow of delegation is always downwards
  • Delegation is not passing the buck
  • Accountability flows upwards → subordinate is accountable to his or her superior

What should one delegate?

  • Routine, recurring and occasional
  • Other team members can do as well or even better
  • Develop the skills and knowledge of team members
  • Provide a challenge that will train and extend a team member’s skills and experience that increase the delegate’s job interest
  • Tasks that need a special skill that you don’t have but someone does or can develop easily
  • Things that don’t require your personal input, provided they are not the boring tasks

Delegation Process

What to delegate?

  • What skills are needed for this job
  • What resources are needed

Who to delegate to?

  • Previously expressed desires by staff
  • Staff member’s availability
  • Level of assistance a staff member will need from you
  • How long the staff member has been on the job
  • The number of previous assignments you have delegate to that person
  • Possibility of diving the tasks between 2 or more people
  • Who wants to learn it
  • You want to prepare for promotion or to be your successor
  • Who is already able and willing to do it
  • Delegation must be done in a level wise manner

Delegate

How does one tell what they should do

  • Background
  • Objectives
  • Reason
  • Time frames/Timings
  • Resources and Administration
  • Progress
  • Always ask if there

Deciding how to assign

  • Explicit Assignments - be specific
  • Outcome Assignments - just tell what you want as outcome
  • Requests
  • Implied Requests (Indirect - maybe in the case of seniors or something)
  • Open Requests

Monitor Results

  • Level 5 → I’ll walk you through it
  • Level 4 → Let’s talk it through first
    • Everything should be checked with the supervisor
  • Level 3 → Check back first
    • Only important things will be checked by the supervisor
  • Level 2 → Keep me informed
    • Delegate can do what he wants but keep the manager posted
  • Level 1 → Just do it
    • There’s nothing to be informed to the supervisor
    • High amount of trust and responsibility in the delegatee

What should one not delegate?

  • Tasks that are difficult to perform
  • Tasks that are unpleasant to perform
  • Tasks that are to be performed for the first time
    • Caution: have the right kind of support system and expectations
  • Tasks that involve high risk
  • Tasks that are confidential
  • Tasks that need one’s special authority
  • Tasks that are personal responsibility

Why are managers shy of delegating?

  • They believe it takes longer
  • They do not want to lose some jobs that they enjoy
  • The next level lacks capability experience
  • They fear risk of losing control

Why employees may not welcome delegation?

  • They do not want to assume ambiguous or unclear duties and responsibilities
  • They are afraid of failure
  • They want to increase job stress
  • Delegators responsibility to ensure you do well

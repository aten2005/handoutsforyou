Communication Types

Based on Context

  • Intrapersonal → self talk
  • Interpersonal → communication between two parties
  • Group → 3 to 8 people
  • Public → large number of audience, public focuses on the speaker. Might use feedback or might not use feedback
  • Mass → large number of audience, mass focuses on the audience, advertisements, etc. There’s no feedback but has media involved

Based on Nature

  • Verbal - Oral and Written
  • Non Verbal

Understanding your Audience

  • You should know audiences’ Attitude, beliefs, values
  • How big is the audience?
  • you can’t change value in general → not through communication
  • What are their backgrounds?
  • Do they already know about your topic?
  • Will other materials be presented or available
  • How much time is allotted for your presentation, or how much space do you have for your written document
  • In Ivy League institutions, we can’t think of teaching 2000 students at once
  • If they already have knowledge, you need to see how much
  • Poor communicator if you are not able to cover all the topics you want to share in the presentation.
  • Pitching for startups → 15min doesn’t mean you prepare for 15min. You should go with multiple plans. He can either increase or decrease the plan

Effective Speaking

  • Communication is effective only it is heard by the right people
  • Confidence + audience + clarity + purpose + preparation
  • Chief means by which organizations work on a day to day basis
  • The way you speak reflects your intelligence, ability
  • How to make a request such that it’s hard for the other person to say NO

Guidelines

  • Determine purpose of your communication
  • Consider issues of time and space
  • Adapt to your listeners
  • Use appropriate vocabulary
  • Practice voice control
  • Use appropriate gestures
  • Organize your presentation

Listening

  • The opposite of talking isn’t listening → it is the wait to talk
  • The better you listen, the more you will be listened to

Listening Positions

Active vs Passive

Active

  • Hear without interruption
  • Ensure you understood
  • It has to be verified that what you heard and understood is exactly same as what the sender meant
  • Before responding, you have to verify your understanding
  • No assumptions as a receiver
  • Restate (word by word without missing anything), Paraphrase and reflect

Passive

  • No verification
  • you assume you understood

Empathetic vs Critical

Empathetic

  • Understand feelings and the meaning behind it

Critical

  • Least bothered about emotions
  • Only bothered about content

Expansive vs Reductive

Expansive

  • Span of tension
  • Willing to listen for a long time

Reductive

  • Many things are said
  • only certain things are attracted to you
  • Other things pass by
  • Could be based on context, problems
  • Reductive is nice but don’t be always reductive.

Listening Styles

  • People oriented
  • Content oriented → logic rationality
  • Action oriented → interested in knowing about what will be done
  • Time oriented → you wrap it up, time pressure based situations
  • One of them would be the predominant listening styles
  • Figure out how to be able to talk to all of these

Introduction

  • A grievance is anything that a person resents
  • You should know how to prevent and how to handle
  • Strike - higher level of complaint
  • If you want to resolve grievance, you need to know the cause of it
  • Any grievance - first try informally
  • Never take an unhappy employee for granted

Effect of Unsolved Grievances

  • Lower morale
  • Lack of interest in the job
  • Increase in absenteeism
  • Wastage of time
  • Fall of quality
  • Lower productivity
  • Bickering and quarrels
  • Poor employee relations
  • Extension of ambit
  • Sprouting → different groups in the organization

Sources of Grievance

  • Contract related
  • Job related - getting bullied, working conditions
  • Off the job conditions - not giving resources, fatigue, lack of patience, error in performance

Steps for Informal Grievance Handling

  • Locate the grievance
  • hear the complaint
    • Be non judgmental and find out frustrations etc. and listen to multiple sides of the story
  • Interview
    • Tell them you are willing to listen
    • You should not be reactive
  • Get the facts
    • Don’t assume anything
    • Check your understanding
    • tell when you will get back to them also in terms of when is the response
  • Diagnose the cause
    • Agree completely with the complainant
    • Do not agree to the employee completely
    • Need to be Investigate properly
    • Agree to some parts but not all parts
  • Plan the solution
  • Apply the solution
  • Check results

Some Pertinent Issues

  • Training and delegated authority
  • Importance of early settlement → quicker response always better
  • Regarding grievances as opportunities
  • Watching for warning signs
  • Learning from grievances
  • Understanding the Union Representative’s role → more legal implications for the opponent → formal mechanism

Do(s) in Handling Grievances

  • Investigate as if it will result in an arbitration
  • Discuss and give a full hearing
  • Determine if the grievant was the proper individual to have filled the grievance
  • Visit the work area of the grievant
  • Determine if there were any witnesses
  • Determine if the company has been consistent
  • Determine if there has been equal treatment of employees
  • Examine the grievant’s personal record
  • Evaluate any political connotations of the grievance
  • Permit a full hearing of the issues
  • Admin your errors and take corrective action
  • Advise the grievant of your corrective action
  • Hold your grievance discussions privately
  • Use grievance settlements to reinforce your relationships
  • Use the grievance meeting as another avenue of communication
  • Treat union reps and employees as people
  • Help employees understand economic realities
  • Know your employees as individuals. Demonstrate qualities of leadership to your subordinates
  • Handle discipline situations correctly from beginning to end
  • Control your emotions, your remarks, your behavior
  • Fully inform your own supervisor of grievance matters

Don’t(s) in Handling Grievances

  • Argue the merits of the grievance first if the grievance is untimely
  • Make agreements with individuals that are inconsistent with the labor agreement
  • Apply the grievance remedy to an improper grievant
  • Hold back the remedy if the company is wrong
  • Admit to the binding effect of a past practice
  • Settle grievance based on what is fair
  • Make mutual consent agreements regarding future actions
  • Argue grievance issues off work premises
  • Commit the company in areas beyond your limits of responsibility or familiarity
  • Give away your copy of the written grievance
  • Settle a grievance while in doubt
  • Support another supervisor in a hopeless case
  • Refer the grievant to a different forum of adjudication
  • Overlook the precedent value of prior grievance settlements
  • Give long written grievance answers
  • Trade a grievance settlement for a grievance withdrawal
  • Inconvenience production operations to facilitate grievance handling
  • Deny grievances on the premise that the management has tied your hands

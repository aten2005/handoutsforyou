Introduction

  • Leader creates the environment within which team members’ work and the right climate drives performance
  • Emotions can help leaders think and make good decisions and forms the basis of good quality relationships
  • Leaders influence the team’s mood and the team’s mood drives performance
  • Leaders high in EI have a solid level of self awareness about the link between their behaviors and how it makes others feel and importantly, perform
  • If you want to hone your emotional skills, you will need to be able to handle a lot of skills
  • They should be able to handle conflicts
  • Understanding and regulating your own emotions as well as other’s emotions
  • Daniel Goleman defines emotional intelligence as “understanding one’s own feelings, empathy for the feelings of others and regulation of emotion in a way that enhances living”
  • The ability to monitor one’s own and other’s emotions to discriminate among them and use the information to guide one’s thinking

Daniel Goleman’s EI Model

  • Model stemmed from his experience and research, which focused on behaviors, emotions and the brain
  • The most widely accepted and used EI model
  • Involves a range of competencies which are broken down into skill sets and which together form the picture of a person’s level of EI
  • Connection between brain, emotions and behavior
  • These competencies are not considered to be innate. Instead they must be developed over time in order to develop and improve performance
  • INNATE means born with

Emotional Leadership Styles

Authoritative/Visionary Leader

  • They are inspiring and they move people toward a common goal
  • Tell their teams where they are all going, but not how they are going to get there - they leave it up to the team members to find their way to the common goal
  • Empathy is the most important aspect of their leadership
  • When to use it?
    • When the organization needs a new vision or a dramatic new direction
    • When you are working with a team less experienced than you are
  • Till 2010 → BITS known for best teaching university
  • But they changed their vision towards best research based university → does not mean teaching is not important
  • How do we develop it?
    • Focus on increasing your expertise, vision, self confidence and empathy
    • Get excited about change and let your team see your enthusiasm
    • Improve your presentation skills especially communication skills

Coaching Leader

  • Connects people’s personal goals with the organization goals
  • Focuses on developing others for future success
  • Having in depth conversations with employees on long term life goals and how these connect with organization’s mission
  • When to use it?
    • When you have a team member who needs help building long term skills or if you feel that he or she is adrift
  • How do we develop it?
    • Learn how to engage in informal coaching and mentoring
  • Coach will identify areas of problems but while mentors are not specific but rather just facilitate
  • Know your people well

Affiliative Leader

  • Promotes harmony within the team
  • Connects people together, encouraging inclusion and resolving conflict
  • Value the emotions of others
  • When to use it?
    • There is team tension or conflict when trust has been broken or if the team needs to be motivated through a stressful time
  • How to develop it?
    • You should learn how to resolve conflicts and how to be optimistic

Democratic Leader

  • Focuses on collaboration
  • Actively seek input from their teams
  • When to use it?
    • Need to get your team on board or build consensus
    • You need team input and they have the capability, motivation and knowledge
  • How to develop it?
    • Involve your team in problem solving and decision making
    • You should learn active listening and facilitation skills
    • Group Discussions

Pacesetting Leader

  • Focuses on performance and meeting goals
  • Expect excellence from their teams
  • Doesn’t coddle poor performers
  • When to use it?
    • When you need to get high quality results from a motivated team, quickly
  • How to develop it?
    • Learn how to improve the quality of your team’s work
    • Train your people
    • Work on your motivation skills

Coercive/Commanding Leader

  • Use an autocratic approach to leadership
  • Often depends on orders, threat of punishment and tight control
  • Often misused
  • When to use it?
    • Used in crisis situations, to jump start fast paced change and with problem employees
  • How to develop it?
    • Learn how to manage crises, think on your feet and make good decisions under pressure
    • Here crisis leads to forcing them to work due to the fact that we cannot use any of the previous types

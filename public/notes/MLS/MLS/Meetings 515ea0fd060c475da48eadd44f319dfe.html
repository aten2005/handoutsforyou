Organizing Meetings

  • What is the purpose of the meeting?
    • You should be able to write the purpose of the meeting in less than 25 words
  • Is the meeting necessary?
    • Can it be achieved by other means
    • Can I send the notice
    • If there are alternate methods, we will use that
  • Who must attend the meeting?
    • Who should attend and who should not attend
    • They should be able to make worth while contributions
  • What should be the size of the meeting?
    • There is no way to organize a meet with a lot of people where it becomes hard for everyone to come
    • If there’s a small meet, then hard to reach consensus
  • When should a meeting be scheduled
    • Not post lunch → sleepy
    • Not before close of the day
    • Critical means no time
  • Where should the meeting be held?
    • Conference room
    • Not necessary with employees
    • Could be consultants
  • How are the participants to be informed about the meetings?
    • Through an agenda
    • List of items to be discussed in the meeting
    • Come prepared → highly productive

Determining the Agenda

  • Always have a written agenda and also provide a copy to each participant
  • Agenda must contain a one sentence objective
  • Put the topics in the order of their priority
  • Limit the agenda

How to Run a Meeting?

  • Begin the meet with an outline of the purpose
  • Announce the adjournment time
  • Begin on time
  • Round robin approach → give everyone an opportunity
  • Leaders don’t dominate
  • Delegate meeting leadership → circulate the role of the chairperson with multiple people
  • Keep discussion on track
  • Summarize the decisions
  • Minutes of the meeting
    • Always can go back and refer it
    • Useful for following meetings

Participating in Meetings

  • Be prepared and do your homework
  • Get involved early
    • Don’t wait too long to contribute
    • Be confident, don’t take that risk
    • There are a hell lot of problems
  • Be an active participant
  • Good manners
    • Report on time
    • Respect the chair
    • Be polite
    • Be a good listener
    • Display positive attitude
    • Be constructive in criticisms → always easy to find faults. maybe give nice feedback
    • Work towards a fair decision
    • Present your points of view lucidly and logically → try to convey message in crisp manner
    • Dissent only when you must

Problems in Meetings

  • Convergent Thinking →
  • Tendency to avoid argument and to rush to a conclusion →
  • Influence of loud and fast speakers →they tend to dominate and displayed in a very loud voice
  • Vague goals →
  • Domination by the leader and inability to keep the meeting on track →

Tactical Seating

    • Don’t make them sit together

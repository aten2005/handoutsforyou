Role of a Leader

What is Leadership? (Clearly not Political Leaders)

  • Is the ability to inspire confidence and support among the people who are needed to achieve organizational goals
  • Is defined as influencing people so that they will strive willingly and enthusiastically towards the achievement of group goals
  • Also should influence only when there is a need for them to do it else no
  • Willingness to work with zeal and confidence
  • Is the initiative or ability of a person to mobilize and/or influence internal and external stakeholders to achieve desired results
    • within the organization
    • external to the organization
  • It is action, not a position
  • Matter of how to be and not how to do
  • Leader has a capacity to make a compelling vision → by definition, leader has a vision
  • Leader is character and is someone who influences others
    • Highest order of integrity
    • Less self triumph → will not flex but if need comes they will show their capabilities
    • Making them to believe that it’s possible to make something happen
    • Having vision is not the issue, but making people believe in it is where the difference comes into picture
  • Leaders pull rather than push followers away
  • Punishment is not the way to go about it
  • Leaders inspire people and empower them and have the courage to accept responsibility
  • Charismatic relation: ability to attract people based on your vision
  • You are not only responsible for yourself but for a lot of others

Manager vs leader

  • Manager → structured, controlled, analytical, orderly and rule oriented → stable
  • Leader → experimental, visionary, unstructured, flexible
  • Manager → Administers, Maintains, Accepts reality, focuses on systems, relies on control, focus on the bottom line (profits), imitates (some other company, department is doing)
  • Leader → innovates, develops, investigates, focuses on people, inspires trust, focus on the horizon (future - long term), originates → looking for growth
  • Devil’s Advocate :) → why it cannot be done?
  • System → SOPs, Structure
  • What else? → focus on people, ideas, utilization

Leadership Styles → Kurt Lewin

Autocratic

  • Concerned on their future
  • Only focus on results
  • PDC → Planning, Directing, Controlling

Democratic

  • Very positive → their needs and their wants are being taken care of
  • Taking input ≠ agreeing with them → still a form of democracy
  • Conflicts are not suppressed → rather manage these conflicts
  • Not about pin pointing problems → rather search for solution

Delegative

  • Neither bothered about results nor bother about people
  • Complete freedom to the followers and subordinates in the team
  • You decide what is to be done, you decide how it should be done
  • Follower goes with a problem → he will make the follower think through and let him get the solution himself → will act as facilitator
  • Empowerment → the follower will take responsibility
  • Build bridges between teams

About the Styles

  • No particular style is the best
  • In case of fire → democratic will not work → in crisis → autocratic style way better
  • Delegative is applicable when you know your subordinates are highly experienced
  • We all have predominant leadership styles
  • Some of us are autocratic, democratic or delegative
  • How to move from one style to another style effectively
  • You should effectively use all of them

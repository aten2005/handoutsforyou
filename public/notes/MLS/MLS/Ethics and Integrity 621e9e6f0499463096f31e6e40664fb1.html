Introduction

  • Leaders influence the lives of others → has a moral and ethical responsibility for their well being
  • Must use authority for the common good
  • Leaders should be sensitive to how their leadership affects the well being of others
  • Focus on the properties and characteristics of ethical leadership
  • Probability for you to implement these characteristics will go up

Ethical Leadership

  • Process by which a good person rightly influences others to accomplish a common good to make the world better, fairer
  • It is the influence of a moral person who moves others to do the right thing in the right way for the right reason
  • In leadership ethics has to do with what leaders do and the nature of these leader’s behavior including their motives

Factors related to ethical leadership

Character

  • Core of who you are as a person, forms the centerpiece of a person’s values
  • Enables a leader to maintain his or her core ethical values even in times of immense adversity
  • Learn to strengthen and develop
  • Practiced over time, good values become habitual, part of people themselves
  • 6 Pillars of Character
    • Trustworthiness
      • Be honest
      • Be reliable
      • Have the courage to do the right thing
      • Don’t deceive, cheat or steal
      • Build a good reputation
    • Respect
      • Be tolerant of differences
      • Use good manners
      • Be considerate of others
      • Work out disagreements
    • Responsibility
      • Do your job
      • Persevere
      • Think before you act
      • Consider the consequences
      • Be accountable for your choices → have self control
      • Responding as per the expectation
    • Fairness
      • Play by the rules, balance justice and what one feels
      • Be openminded
      • Don’t take advantage of others
      • Don’t blame others
    • Caring
      • Be kind
      • Be compassionate
      • Forgive others → it may always not be possible so try to always be forgiving in nature
      • Help people in need don’t expect things in return
    • Citizenship
      • Share with your community
      • Get involved
      • Respect authority
      • Protect the environment

Values

  • Ideas, beliefs, and modes of action that people find worthwhile or desirable
  • Ethical
    • Similar to notion of character
  • Moral
    • Concerned with means or actions a leader takes
  • End
    • Describe the outcomes/goals a leader seeks to achieve
  • Be sensitive to other’s values regardless of if they respect your values or not

Actions

  • Refer to ways a leader goes about accomplishing goals
  • Ethical leaders use moral means to achieve their goals
  • Ask “Do the ends justify means”
  • Ethical principles guides how a leader acts toward others
  • Do not do biased discrimination → it should only be based on merit and not any arbitrary characteristics
  • Showing respect by treating people as unique and value others’ ideas
  • Serving others by promoting the best interest of others and act in interest of others even when it may run contrary to his or her self interests → altruism → others interest comes first
  • Be follower centered
  • Show justice and treat all followers in an equal manner. When individuals are treated differently, the grounds for different treatment must be clear, reasonable, and based on sound moral values
  • Follow golden rule → The way you expect you want to be treated is the way you treat others

Power

  • Capacity to influence or affect others
  • Can be used in positive or destructive way
  • Leaders should not misuse Power
  • How you exercise power is where the problem comes
  • Bases/Sources of Power
    • Referent → Based on follower’s identification and liking for the leader
    • Expert → Based on follower’s perceptions of the leader’s competence
    • Legitimate → Associated with having status or formal job authority
    • Reward → Derived from having the capacity to provide benefits to others
    • Coercive → Derived from being able to penalize or punish others

Goals

  • Goal a leader sets should be just and worthy
  • Must assess the relative value and worth of his or her goals
  • Take others interests into goals
  • Establish goals on which all parties can mutually agree
  • Leaders don’t impose his or her will on others
  • Consensus → is not majority → giving an opportunity for minority and they are heard completely yet for the benefit of the larger group the minority agrees to the majority
  • Everyone agrees → unanimous

Honesty

  • Honest is synonymous with being ethical
  • Dishonesty creates distrust, has negative effect on a leader’s interpersonal relationships
  • Being honest → telling the truth, being open → representing reality as fully and completely as possible
  • Strike a balance between being open and candid and deciding what is appropriate to disclose in a particular situation
  • Actively lying << withholding information which you are supposed to tell
